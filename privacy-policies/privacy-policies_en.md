AcmeVPN is operated by Acme Networks, Inc. (hereinafter referred to as "we," "the Company," or "AcmeVPN"). This Privacy Policy applies to all services we provide and is intended to comply with major global data protection regulations.

In accordance with data protection laws applicable to various jurisdictions, we process your personal data on the following legal bases and within a limited scope:
- Contract Performance: To provide VPN services, manage your account, and process payments.
- Legitimate Interests: To improve our services, ensure security, and prevent fraud.
- Legal Obligation: To comply with applicable laws and regulations, tax, or accounting requirements.

For California residents, we shall also comply with the California Consumer Privacy Act/California Privacy Rights Act (hereinafter "CCPA/CPRA"), committing not to sell or share your personal data without consent and providing you with an "opt-out" mechanism.

## 3. Principle of Data Minimization 
AcmeVPN adheres strictly to the principles of data minimization and purpose limitation:
- We collect only the minimum data necessary to provide VPN services.
- Data is collected solely for explicit and legitimate purposes.
- We regularly review the necessity of collected data and promptly delete or anonymize unnecessary data.

## 4. Data Collection Policy
When you use the AcmeVPN website or client application, your internet data will be protected by advanced encryption technologies. Your online traffic shall be invisible to Internet Service Providers (ISPs), third-party eavesdroppers, and unauthorized entities.

### 4.1 No-Log Policy Commitment
AcmeVPN strictly adheres to a No-Log Policy, meaning that during your use of VPN services, we do not collect, record, or store any data that could identify or trace individual users or reconstruct their online activities, including:
- Domain names and destination IP addresses you accessed through VPN services.
- DNS query records.
- Connection timestamps and session duration.
- Traffic destinations and data transmission content.
- For EU users, we further commit under GDPR not to establish identifiable user activity databases.

**Important Notice**:
Our No-Log Policy applies solely to the VPN service. For operational security purposes related to our website and client application, we may collect limited access log information from these platforms. This data is strictly separated from VPN usage logs both technically and in scope.

### 4.2 Necessary Technical Data Collection
To maintain service quality, security, and performance optimization, we only collect the following necessary anonymous technical data. Such data consists solely of aggregated anonymous statistics and does not involve your personal identity or specific online activities, including:

#### 4.2.1 VPN Service-Related Data
- Traffic Statistics: Account-level cumulative traffic statistics (not linked to the specific time or content).
- Device Connections: Number of simultaneously connected devices (used to enforce service plan limits).
- Server Performance Metrics: Server performance data such as server load, bandwidth usage, and country/region of source IP address (used solely to recommend optimal server locations or improve routing performance, completely anonymized, not linked to specific users, and not used for profiling or automated decision-making).
- Application Stability Data: Crash reports and error diagnostic information of the Application (containing no personal data).

#### 4.2.2 Access Logs from Website/Client Application 
- Access Time: Used for website performance analysis and security auditing.
- Browser Information: Used to ensure website compatibility and optimize your experience.
- Pages Visited: Used to improve website structure and content.
- Access Source Information (e.g., source IP addresses): Used solely for website security monitoring, abuse prevention, route optimization, and localization (e.g., language display).

**Important Notice**:
- Access logs are completely separated from VPN service usage logs both technically and operationally.
- Access source information shall not be linked to your activities during VPN usage.
- Access logs shall be anonymized promptly after collection.
- Access logs are retained for no more than 30 days and are automatically deleted or anonymized upon expiration.

### 4.3 Anti-Abuse Measures
We implement the following measures to prevent service abuse, which do not affect your privacy:
- Limits on concurrent device connections.
- Detection of abnormal traffic patterns (based on anonymous statistics).
- Monitoring of technical indicators related to account usage (e.g., concurrent connections, frequency of abnormal requests), without involving content-level data.
We may collaborate with third-party security providers (e.g., DDoS protection services) for protection, but we shall not share identifiable user activity data. Third-party providers only access necessary anonymous technical data.

## 7. Cookies Policy

### 7.1 What Are Cookies
Cookies are small text files stored on your device that websites use to remember information about your visits. The AcmeVPN website uses cookies to enhance your experience, and the cookie types may evolve as the website is updated and improved.

### 7.2 Types of Cookies We Use

**Essential Cookies**:
- Used for basic website functions such as maintaining login status and security verification.
- These cookies are critical for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled.

**Analytics Cookies (Google Analytics)**:
- Used to measure website traffic and analyze performance to help us improve website functions.
- Collects anonymous visit data that does not include personal identification information.

**Marketing Cookies, Affiliate and Referral Cookies**:
- Used to identify users who visit our website through partner referrals, enabling us to pay commissions to relevant partners.
- Tracks referral sources and evaluates marketing campaign effectiveness.

**Functional and Personalization Cookies**:
- Used to remember your preferences including but not limited to default language settings.
- Enhances personalized user experience.

### 7.3 Third-Party Cookies
Some cookies we use are provided by third-party providers:
- Google Analytics: for website analytics services.
- Stripe: for secure payment processing.
- Cloudflare: for network and security services.

### 7.4 Cookies Management and User Control
- Browser Settings: You may manage or disable cookies via browser settings.
- Choice Rights: You have the right to refuse non-essential cookies, retaining only the essential cookies necessary for website operation.
- Change Preferences Anytime: You may change your cookie preferences at any time.
- Impact Notices: Disabling certain cookies may affect some website functions, such as language preference memory.
- Specific Jurisdictional Notes: For EU users, all non-essential cookies require your prior explicit consent. For California residents, you may opt out of advertising-related cookies via "Do Not Sell/Share" identifiers.

### 7.5 Cookies Retention Periods
- Session Cookies: Automatically deleted upon browser closure.
- Persistent Cookies: Retained for different periods depending on specific purposes (usually 1 to 24 months).
- Third-Party Cookies: Follow the retention policies of respective third-party providers.

## 8. User Rights
According to data protection laws applicable in your jurisdiction, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

You have the right to know and access what personal data of yours we have collected and how we use this information.

### 8.2 Right to Rectification
You have the right to request us to correct/rectify/supplement/update any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.

### 8.3 Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)
You have the right to request deletion of your personal data, provided that:
- The personal data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected.
- You withdraw your consent and there is no other legal basis for processing.
- The personal data has been processed unlawfully.

### 8.4 Right to Restrict Processing
You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.

### 8.5 Right to Data Portability
You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and to transmit that data to another controller.

### 8.6 Right to Object
You have the right to object to our processing of your personal data based on legitimate interests for reasons related to your particular situation.

### 8.7 Right to Withdraw Consent
If our data processing activities are based on your prior consent, you may withdraw such consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing activities conducted prior to withdrawal. However, withdrawal may result in certain services becoming unavailable.

You may submit feedback through the help center of the AcmeVPN App or website, or send an email to support@acmevpn.io.

### 8.8 Right to Refuse Sale/Sharing
For California residents, you also have the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal data. Should we provide identification or tracking data to third-party providers for the purpose of targeted advertising on their products based on our own product or service promotions, we shall prominently display a "Do Not Sell/Share" identifier and provide reasonable opt-out mechanisms.

### 8.9 How to Exercise Your Rights
We respect and comply with the data subject rights granted by applicable law. To exercise any of the above rights, please submit feedback through the help center of the AcmeVPN App or website, or send an email to support@acmevpn.io.

To protect your privacy and account security, we shall conduct reasonable identity verification before processing requests for access/deletion/correction. If you authorize a third party to submit a request on your behalf, we may require that third party to provide written authorization or verified proof of agency.

**Specific Jurisdictional Notes**:
To accommodate statutory time limits in different jurisdictions, we shall adhere to the following time limits for your requests:
- For EU users, we shall typically respond to your requests within 1 month (30 days). In complex cases, we may extend this period by up to 2 months (an additional 60 days) as permitted by law. Should an extension occur, we shall explain the reasons and inform you within 1 month.
- For California residents, we shall respond to verifiable consumer requests within 45 days. If an extension is necessary, we may extend the response period by an additional 45 days (totaling 90 days). Should an extension occur, we shall promptly notify you and explain the reasons.
- For users in other jurisdictions, we shall respond promptly based on applicable jurisdictional regulations and process requests within a reasonable timeframe. If delays occur due to cross-border issues or identity verification, we shall inform you of the reasons and expected timeframes.

## 14. Cross-Border Data Transfers

### 14.1 Cross-Border Transfer Description
To facilitate AcmeVPN’s services and operations, we may store, access, and transfer your personal data to servers located outside your country/region. We shall thoroughly assess all circumstances related to cross-border data transfers and implement appropriate safeguards to ensure your personal data receives protection no less stringent than the standards required in your country/region. For example, if your personal data is transferred to countries/regions outside the EU, we shall ensure such transfers are made only to countries for which the European Commission has issued an adequacy decision for the receiving countries, or we shall use the European Commission’s approved Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) to process such transfers of your personal data. Where the legal risks in the receiving countries are higher, we shall also take supplementary measures such as end-to-end encryption, access control, and periodic risk assessments.

### 14.2 Transfer Safeguards
For cross-border data transfers, we implement the following safeguards:
- Encrypted Transfer: All cross-border data transfers utilize specific encryption protocols.
- Security Assessment: Periodically evaluate the security environment of data transfer destinations.
- Minimized Transfer: Transfer only necessary data and control the scope of transfer strictly.
- Compliance Monitoring: Ensuring all data transfers comply with relevant privacy laws.

### 14.3 User Control 
You have the right to know the countries or regions where your data is transferred to and the corresponding safeguards.
